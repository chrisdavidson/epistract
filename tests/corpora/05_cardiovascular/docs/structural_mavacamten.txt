Title: Mavacamten (Camzyos) — Structural and Pharmacological Profile
Source: Compiled from PubChem CID 135562068, DrugBank DB16638, and published data

COMPOUND IDENTITY:
Mavacamten (MYK-461, Camzyos) is a first-in-class selective cardiac myosin inhibitor.
CAS Registry Number: 1642288-47-8
SMILES: CC(C1=CC=CC=C1)NC(=O)N1CCC(CC1)OC1=NC=NC(=C1)NCC1=CC=CC=C1
Molecular Formula: C25H28N4O2
Molecular Weight: 428.52 g/mol

MECHANISM:
Mavacamten selectively inhibits cardiac myosin ATPase by stabilizing an auto-inhibited
(super-relaxed) state of beta-cardiac myosin (MYH7). This reduces the number of
myosin cross-bridges in the sarcomere, decreasing cardiac contractility.

Target: Beta-cardiac myosin heavy chain (MYH7, UniProt: P12883)
Binding site: Allosteric site on the myosin motor domain
IC50: 0.3 μM (bovine cardiac myosin ATPase)

SELECTIVITY:
>100-fold selective for cardiac myosin over fast skeletal myosin (MYH1).
Does not inhibit smooth muscle myosin.

PATHWAY:
Mavacamten acts on the sarcomere — the fundamental contractile unit of cardiac muscle.
In HCM, mutations in MYH7 or MYBPC3 cause excessive cross-bridge formation,
leading to hypercontractility and dynamic left ventricular outflow tract (LVOT)
obstruction.

KEY CLINICAL TRIALS:
- EXPLORER-HCM (NCT03470545): Phase III, mavacamten vs placebo in oHCM
  Primary endpoint met: LVOT gradient reduction + symptom improvement
  47% of mavacamten patients achieved composite endpoint vs 22% placebo
- VALOR-HCM (NCT04349072): Phase III, mavacamten in patients eligible for
  septal reduction therapy. 82% no longer met SRT criteria at 16 weeks.

REGULATORY:
FDA approved April 28, 2022 for symptomatic obstructive HCM (NYHA class II-III).
REMS program required due to risk of heart failure with reduced ejection fraction.

SAFETY:
Key adverse events: dizziness (11%), syncope (4%), atrial fibrillation (4%)
Black box warning: Risk of heart failure due to systolic dysfunction.
Requires echocardiographic monitoring of LVEF before and during treatment.
Contraindicated: LVEF < 55%, concurrent use of strong CYP2C19 inhibitors.

METABOLISM:
Mavacamten is primarily metabolized by CYP2C19 and CYP3A4.
CYP2C19 poor metabolizers have ~2-fold higher exposure.
Strong CYP2C19 inhibitors (fluconazole, fluvoxamine) are contraindicated.
